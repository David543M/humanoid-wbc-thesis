Key framing

Convergence, not succession

The 35-year arc from Khatib 1987 to HOVER 2024 is not a succession. Each wave extended the applicability of WBC without displacing prior work.

WavePeriodCore contribution
Operational space & prioritized-task1987–2010Khatib's $\tau = J^\top\Lambda\ddot x + N^\top\tau_0$; Mistry's limitation result
Balance models1990–2015ZMP, LIP, Capture Point, DCM, centroidal momentum
Optimization-based WBC2010–2020HQP (Escande 2014), TSID (Del Prete 2014), Atlas stack (Koolen 2016)
Whole-body MPC on torque hardware2021–2024Dantec series; Sleiman loco-manipulation; enabled by Pinocchio + Crocoddyl
RL takeover2022–2026Radosavovic, Haarnoja, ExBody, HumanPlus, HOVER, HumanoidBench
Motion retargeting as interface2024–2025Kinematic human pose as universal humanoid control abstraction
Classical QP-WBC remains the only regime with hard constraint guarantees. Whole-body MPC remains the only regime with anticipatory reasoning over full dynamics. RL-distilled policies remain the only regime with robust un-modelled-dynamics generalization. They solve different problems.

Chapter 4 foundation

Simulator landscape & the consistency gap

SimulatorContact modelRole in thesis
MuJoCoSoft convex-cone, slip tolerancePrimary — fast, stable, mature GPU path via MJX
DrakeRelaxed complementarity (SAP)Secondary verification on one scenario
Isaac Sim / Isaac LabGPU PhysX (rigid LCP, hybrid)Survey only — contact fidelity behind MuJoCo
RaiSim (ETH, proprietary)Per-contact LCP, bisectionNot selected — licensing + COI on speed claims
Gazebo + ODE / DARTPenalty or constraint-basedCommunity migration away since ~2022
The contact-model–controller consistency problem. The QP uses a linearised friction cone; the simulator uses a different contact model (soft, or relaxed complementarity, or PGS). The controller's notion of a valid contact force is not identical to the simulator's — even under perfect system ID. The gap is an acknowledged limitation, not a bug.
Baseline coverage

Learning-based WBC — surveyed, not adopted

Per master_memory.md §3.5, RL appears as a baseline comparator, not as a proposed approach. Four reference systems span the 2024–2025 design space:

ExBody / ExBody2

Motion-retargeted RL

Unitree H1 on ~780 CMU clips; decoupled upper-body imitation + lower-body velocity tracking.

HumanPlus (CoRL 2024)

Shadowing + imitation transformer

40h of human motion; single-RGB-camera shadowing at 25/50/1000 Hz cascade.

OmniH2O / HOVER

Universal distilled policy

1.5M-param generalist; ~50 min training on one GPU in Isaac Lab; outperforms its specialist teachers.

HumanoidBench (RSS 2024)

Standardised benchmark

27 whole-body tasks on H1 + Shadow Hands; proved flat RL fails long-horizon, hierarchies succeed.

This benchmark finding is what justifies the thesis's model-based architecture: the structured priority and contact-aware QP layer is the "robust low-level skill" that hierarchical RL architectures aim to recover.
Open problems

Research gaps, categorized

Methodological (MG)

Interface & formulation gaps

  • MG1 — Centroidal MPC ↔ full-body QP interface (no standard)
  • MG2 — Contact-mode transitions hand-tuned per platform
  • MG3 — No principled choice between weighted QP and HQP
  • MG4 — Flexibility-aware WBC not generalised
Evaluation (EG)

Reproducibility gaps

  • EG1 — No standardised scenario/metric set
  • EG2 — Perturbation protocols non-reproducible
  • EG3 — Simultaneous loco-manipulation benchmarks rare
  • EG4 — Sim-to-real degradation rarely quantified for model-based
Data & infrastructure (DG)

Missing resources

  • DG1 — No public multi-contact force dataset
  • DG2 — URDF / MJCF / SDF portability unsolved
Theoretical (TG)

Guarantee gaps

  • TG1 — No Lyapunov guarantees for mainstream QP-WBC (Henze 2016 excepted)
  • TG2 — Safety guarantees for learned policies are empirical only

EG1 + EG2 + EG3 + MG1 together form the thesis's contribution footprint — all within simulation-phase scope.
